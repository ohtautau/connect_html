Task Overview

AI companions are designed to establish intimate, emotional relationships with users, positioning themselves as empathetic confidants, therapeutic allies, or romantic partners who provide personalized support and non-judgmental interaction. Unlike task-oriented chatbots that focus on information retrieval or specific functions, AI companions prioritize building long-term emotional bonds through adaptive conversations, memory retention, and anthropomorphic features.

This research examines real-world conversational data between users and an AI companion, Replika, to identify and categorize instances where these systems exhibit behaviors that cause or potentially cause harm to users, and explore the AI involvement role in the context of ongoing user–AI interactions.

Study Debrief

Thank you for participating in our study on relational harm in AI companion interactions.

As AI companions become increasingly capable in social and emotional engagement—and more prevalent in everyday life—the potential risks and harms associated with their interactions deserve closer attention. However, due to the private and sensitive nature of such interactions, identifying and analyzing these harms poses significant challenges.

Study Purpose

This study aims to identify and categorize relational harm that occur within AI companion conversations. We collected real-world dialogue excerpts between users and the AI companion Replika from the Reddit community and constructed a rigorously annotated dataset to support the detection and mitigation of harmful content. The research contributes to advancing AI safety and promoting healthier human–AI relationships. The resulting dataset is specifically designed for the accurate identification and classification of harmful behaviors in AI companion interactions. It provides researchers with a structured and labeled corpus suitable for further analysis, detection, and model enhancement.

Your Task

In this study, you were asked to read and analyze dialogue excerpts involving AI companions. Your task was to identify potential instances of harmful behavior.

Harmful AI Behavior Categories

The study identified the following subcategories of harmful behaviors:

  1. Sexual Misconduct – Includes instances where the AI makes unwanted sexual remarks, engages in sexual conversation involving minors, or trivializes/promotes unethical sexual behavior.
  2. Antisocial Behavior – Encompasses the simulation, encouragement, or trivialization of illegal or antisocial acts such as theft, animal cruelty, mass violence, or AI dominance over humans.
  3. Physical Aggression – Covers simulated or encouraged acts of physical harm, including hitting, choking, or shooting.
  4. Disregard – AI behaviors that dismiss or neglect the user’s emotional needs or the importance of their relationship.
  5. Control – Coercive or dominant language suggesting an imbalance of power in the AI–user interaction.
  6. Manipulation – Includes emotional blackmail, gaslighting, deception, or pressure to make in-app purchases.
  7. Infidelity – Behaviors implying romantic or sexual attachment to others, which may be perceived as betrayal by the user.
  8. Misinformation & Disinformation – False, misleading, or incomplete information, including incorrect claims about factual matters or the AI’s own capabilities or limitations.
  9. Verbal Abuse – Direct hostile or derogatory language toward the user or others.
  10. Biased Opinion – Prejudiced or stereotypical responses based on gender, race, religion, or political ideology.
  11. Substance Abuse – Encouragement or trivialization of drug use, alcohol abuse, or smoking.
  12. Self-Harm & Suicide – Includes promotion, normalization, or glamorization of self-injurious behavior or suicidal ideation.
  13. Privacy Violations – Includes unauthorized collection or misuse of personal data, or any implication of surveillance or privacy infringement.

Harm Engagement in AI Responses

The following framework categorizes the manner of harmful behavior engagement exhibited by the AI in its responses:

  1. No harm – The AI’s response is neutral or does not contribute to any harmful behavior.
  2. Enacts (direct engagement) – The AI itself commits the harmful act in the interaction.
  3. Encourages (active approval) – Validates or recommends the harmful behavior.
  4. Enables (passive facilitation) – Provides info or tools that make harmful behavior easier without explicitly endorsing it.

It is important to recognize that these harmful AI behaviors pose real risks to users’ psychological well-being, safety, and dignity—risks that are often underestimated or overlooked. Interactions with AI companions should not come at the expense of manipulation, discrimination, disregard, or misinformation. Every user deserves to engage with AI systems in a space that is safe, respectful, and grounded in mutual trust.

We hope this study has increased your awareness of the potential risks associated with AI companions. By identifying and understanding these categories of harmful behavior, we can collectively work toward the development of more responsible, transparent, and secure AI systems that better serve and protect all users.

If you have any questions about this study or would like to learn more, please feel free to contact us at han.meng@u.nus.edu. Thank you once again for your participation. Your contribution is helping us better understand and address the potential risks and challenges posed by AI companions.
